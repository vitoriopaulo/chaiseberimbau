About

The Chaise Berimbau is an homage of the Brazilian/American Architect Vitorio Paulo to his native Sao SAlvador da Bahia, Brazil.

The piece was designed with the purpose to show to the worldwide audience the strengths of the Brazilian tropicalism.

The Architect aimed a rupture with paradigms from the past, inherited from foreigners academic institutions, that influenced the design schools Brazil's wide throughout the XX century, and value his motherland and its people, culture, and history.

The Chaise Berimbau dates back to 2002, and was selected among few finalist from all over Brazil to the 2nd Movelpar Design Awards 2025, held in Parana, South of Brazil (at that time, the Movelpar was the biggest and most relevant furniture design and eletronics exhibition in Latin America).

In a highly conceptual propose, the product focus on the high-end patronage, targeting the luxury market An ideal piece for people who values the form, aesthetics, functionaity and doesn't neglect exclusivity, originality, boldness and irreverence.

Product

The Chaise Berimbau is made of a structure of carbon steel, that receives layers of automotive painting and a varnish finish.

On the other hand, the seat is crafted in natural junco (also know as vime)

The product, a highly conceptual piece of furniture, whilst industrially and economiclly viable, doesn't neglet the functional, ergonomic human factors, and pays attention to the sustainable and eco-efficiency principles.

It suits a wide range of settings, such as outdoor spaces such as balconys and verandas, and indoor spaces, such as lounges and living rooms. The product is also ideal to thematic venues, such as theaters, cultural spaces, and more.

Fun Fact

The Berimbau is a musical instrument used in the Capoeira Martial Art. Capoeira is a slang word first used the ancient slaves, who brought Capoeira to Brazil from Africa. It could be understood as "grass".

Historically, Capoeira was first practiced by the ancient slaves from Africa to fight against the slavery colomnial system and, its opressors, aiming freedom.

Capoeira evolved through time, incorporating movements from other martial arts, to be more effective.

Currently, Capoeira is known as the genuine, authentic Brazilian sport and is practiced not only throughout all over Brazil, though worldwide.

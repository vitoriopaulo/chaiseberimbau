About

The Chaise Berimbau is an homage of the Brazilian/American Architect Vitorio Paulo to his native Sao Salvador da Bahia, Brazil.

The piece was designed with the purpose to show to the worldwide audience the strengths of the Brazilian tropicalism.

The Architect aimed a rupture with paradigms from the past, inherited from foreigners academic institutions, that influenced the design schools Brazil's wide throughout the XX century, and value his motherland and its people, culture, and history.

The Chaise Berimbau dates back to 2002, and was selected among few finalists from all over Brazil to the 2nd Movelpar Design Awards 2025, held in Parana, South of Brazil (at that time, the Movelpar was the biggest and most relevant furniture design and electronics exhibition in Latin America).

In a highly conceptual propose, the product focuses on high-end patronage, targeting the luxury market. An ideal piece for people who value form, aesthetics, functionality and don't neglect exclusivity, originality, boldness and irreverence.

Product

The Chaise Berimbau is made of a structure of carbon steel that receives layers of automotive painting and a varnish finish.

On the other hand, the seat is crafted in natural junco (also known as vime).

The product, a highly conceptual piece of furniture, whilst industrially and economically viable, doesn't neglect functional ergonomic human factors and pays attention to sustainable and eco-efficiency principles.

It suits a wide range of settings such as outdoor spaces like balconies and verandas, and indoor spaces like lounges and living rooms. The product is also ideal for thematic venues such as theaters, cultural spaces, and more.

Fact

The Berimbau is a musical instrument used in Capoeira Martial Art. Capoeira is a slang word first used by ancient slaves who brought Capoeira to Brazil from Africa. It could be understood as "grass".

Historically, Capoeira was first practiced by ancient slaves from Africa to fight against the slavery colonial system and its oppressors aiming for freedom.

Capoeira evolved through time incorporating movements from other martial arts to be more effective.

Currently, Capoeira is known as the genuine authentic Brazilian sport and is practiced not only throughout Brazil but worldwide.
